The Yu Research Group

Welcome to the Yu research group at the University of North Dakota! Our research interests focus on using computational methods to solve challenging problems in chemistry, biology, and materials sciences, inclduing

  • Plasmonic excitations of nanomaterials and application in photocatalysis
  • Bioimaging and biosensing with nanomaterials
  • Photoinduced proton and electron transfer in protein and DNA
  • Structure and dynamics of RNA-binding disorder protein.

We employ quantum chemistry methods and molecular dynamics simulation technique to investigate molecular systems in large scale.

Plasmon and Nanomaterials

Electron-beam induced plasmonic excitations in nanomaterials can be used in photonic device to store and tranfer energy and quantum informan.

Bioimaging and Nanomaterials

Nanogrephene and sillicon materials with different doped atoms can be to select emssion energetic window, ehance the intensity, and increase stability of fluoresence signs.

Proton-Electron Transfer

Photoinduced PT and ET plays essential roles in many biochemical and biophysical process, such as photosynthesis and DNA damage in phototherapy.

Biomolecular Structure

RNA-binding disorder protein displays multiple confirmations. This highly-dynamical feature make the protein regulate the functionality of RNA.
